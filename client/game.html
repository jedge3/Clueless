How to play Clue-Less:

When it is your turn, do any of these 3:

a. Move to a new location.

b. Make a suggestion.

c. Make an accusation.


How to make a move:

When it is your turn, you may move once.

If it your first turn, you must move to the highlighted hallway.

Otherwise, you can move to any of the adjacent hallways/rooms or through a room's passage from one diagonal to another.

You may not move to a hallway occupied by another player.

Select the move button and click on one of the highlighted positions on the board.


How to make a suggestion:

When it is your turn, you can make at most one suggestion.

You must be in a room to make a suggestion.

Click the suggest button then select the character and weapon for the suggestion and press the submit button.

Players will take turns trying to disprove the suggestion. The first player to do so will grant you one of their cards.


How to disprove a suggestion:

When a player makes a suggestion, once it becomes your turn to disprove, if one of your starting cards disproves the suggestion, you must share it with the suggesting player.

Click on the disprove button then select the card you can use to disprove the suggestion.

If you cannot disprove a suggestion, you may use the 'None' card. This will allow the next player to disprove the suggestion.

The suggesting player will be granted a dummy version of this card that cannot be used to disprove suggestions.


How to make an accusation:

When you are confident you know who, where, and how, you may make an accusation.

Click on the accuse button then select the character, room, and weapon for the accusation and press the submit button.

If the accusation is correct, you win the game.

If the accusation is incorrect, you are eliminated and must stick around to disprove player's suggestions.
